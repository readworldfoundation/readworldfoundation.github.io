Our Story

On Novemer 8, 2013...

The City of Tacloban in the Philippines was devastated by super typhoon Haiyan (codename:Yolanda). The strongest storm ever recorded in history flattened the landscape leaving 3,000 people dead from 20-feet storm surge coupled with sustained 355 km/hr winds. The estimated total casualties of in the central Visayas region of the Philippines was 10,000...

making it one of the world's worst natural catastrophe. However, more deaths are expected due to trauma and depression. While the world responded with much generosity, local politics and corruption have caused delay and more misery to thousands of survivors today.

Lethbridge Locals Help Globally

Students in Tacloban City have since returned to school.

A school or organization in Lethbridge or area will be paired with a public school in Tacloban City. Used books and extra school supplies collected by each schools will be sent directly to the adopted schools in the Philippines. Shipping costs for one standard box is $90.

Projects

Since 2014, we have done the following projects to help raise funds.

Adopt A School

Local Lethbridge schools were paired with a public school in the Philippines in 2014. Many local schools and organizations like Lethbridge Public Library continue to support our foundation.

Colour Run

Like running? Have fun with your friends and family! We had a blast with the Catholic Central High School "Colour Run" in 2015.

Lunch For A Cause

In November 2016, society members held a successful fundraiser called "Lunch For A Cause" that featured Filipino cuisine.

Garage Sale

Volunteers have been holding an annual garage sale fundraiser since 2014.
